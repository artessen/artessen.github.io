Short Bio

Dr. Arthur H. Essenfelder is a disaster risk reduction and machine-learning expert working as a scientific project officer at the European Commission - Joint Research Centre (EC-JRC) within the Copernicus Emergency Management Service (CEMS). He is responsible for developing indicators related to drought hazard and risk detection and monitoring, focusing on incorporating machine-learning methods.

Previously, he has acted as the Lead Researcher on Performance Assessment of Disaster Risk Reduction Strategies at the Euro-Mediterranean Centre on Climate Change (CMCC) and as a lecturer at Ca’Foscari University of Venice, Italy, covering topics such as machine-learning models for risk assessments and decision-theory. He has worked with machine-learning development for over 10 years, since his Bachelor's degree in environmental engineering and continuing with his Ph.D. in Science and Management of Climate Change at Ca'Foscari University, Venice.

His main research interests focus on the development and application of AI/ML methods for disaster risk reduction, compound and cascading risk assessments, and crisis management under a perspective of seasonal to decadal time horizons. Dr. Essenfelder has also contributed as an expert to the European Environmental Agency’s European Topic Centre on Climate change impacts, vulnerability and adaptation (ETC-CCA) and as a task leader on disaster impact modelling of the project SaferPLACES (EIT Climate-KIC). He has participated in various scientific conferences and has over 20 scientific publications in international peer-reviewed journals.
