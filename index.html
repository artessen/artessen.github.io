Professional Bio

Dr. Arthur H. Essenfelder is a disaster risk reduction and machine-learning expert working as a scientific project officer at the European Commission - Joint Research Centre (EC-JRC) within the Copernicus Emergency Management Service (CEMS). He is responsible for developing indicators related to drought hazard and risk detection and monitoring, focusing on incorporating machine-learning methods.

Previously, he acted as Lead Researcher on Performance Assessment of Disaster Risk Reduction Strategies at the Euro-Mediterranean Centre on Climate Change (CMCC) and as a lecturer at Ca’Foscari University of Venice. His research focuses on the development and application of AI/ML methods for disaster risk reduction, compound and cascading risk assessments, and crisis management under seasonal to decadal time horizons.

Dr. Essenfelder has also contributed as an expert to the European Environmental Agency’s European Topic Centre on Climate change impacts, vulnerability and adaptation (ETC-CCA) and as a task leader on disaster impact modelling of the project SaferPLACES (EIT Climate-KIC). His main research interests are on development and application of AI/ML methods for disaster risk reduction, on compound and cascading risk assessments, and on crisis management and disaster risk modelling under a perspective of seasonal to decadal time horizons. He has participated in various scientific conferences and has over 20 scientific publications in international peer reviewed journals.
